## PERSONS

### Aditya Chola
- (Kaveri Delta, ~900 AD) - Small-time warlord who became "Big Dog" of Kaveri Delta; ambushed and killed Pallava king for "street cred"; claimed descent from Sangam Cholas
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Parantaka Chola
- (Kaveri Delta/Tamil Nadu, ~900-950 AD) - Son of Aditya; united Delta warlords; defeated Pandyas and Pallavas; tried to raid Sri Lanka; humbled by Rashtrakutas who killed his son Rajaditya
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Rajaditya
- (Tamil Nadu, ~950 AD) - Crown prince, son of Parantaka; killed by Rashtrakutas in battle
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Aditya Karikalan
- (Kaveri Delta, ~960s AD) - Chola prince, murdered by Brahmins from Chola court; Ponniyin Selvan novel set in this period
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Rajaraja Cholan
- (Thanjavur/Tamil Nadu, ~985-1014 AD) - Emerged from chaos; expanded Chola empire in all directions; built Thanjavur Periya Kovil (Big Temple); set up efficient revenue system
- Source: Cholas Part 1, Cholas Part 2

### Rajendra Chola
- (Gangaikondacholapuram/Tamil Nadu, ~1014-1044 AD) - Son of Rajaraja; expanded even further including Bengal coast and overseas; built rival temple at Gangaikondacholapuram
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Uttama Chola
- (Kaveri Delta, ~970-985 AD) - Began the efficient revenue extraction system that Rajaraja perfected
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Sembiyan Mahadevi
- (Tamil Nadu, ~10th century AD) - Renovated nearly 20 temples
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Krishnadevaraya
- (Madurai, ~1529) - Vijayanagara emperor; sent Nayaka governor to Madurai when Pandyas could no longer maintain order
- Source: Madurai Through the Ages

### Thirumalai Nayak
- (Madurai, 1600s) - Most famous Madurai Nayak ruler; built grand palace still standing; rebuilt large portions of Meenakshi Temple
- Source: Madurai Through the Ages

### Vasco da Gama
- (Calicut, 1498) - Portuguese explorer, first European to reach India by sea
- Source: Corporate Warfare

### Nader Shah
- (Delhi, 1739) - Persian invader who looted Mughal treasury; invasion hollowed out Mughal central authority
- Source: How Did a Joint-Stock Company..., Blog 2 (1700-1761)

### Aurangzeb
- (Deccan/Tamil Nadu, 1675-1700) - Mughal emperor, defeated Deccan sultanates, appointed Nizam of Hyderabad
- Source: Blog 1 (17th Century)

### Tej Singh / Desingaraja
- (Gingee, ~1700) - Local chieftain at Gingee Fort, fell into tax arrears, Nawab attacked the fort
- Source: Blog 1 (17th Century), Blog 2 (1700-1761)

### Ramnad Sethupathi
- (Ramnad, 1707) - Declared independence from Madurai Nayaks, emerged as regional power
- (Ramnad, 1801) - Allied with British during Polygar Wars
- Source: Blog 2 (1700-1761), Blog 4 (1761-1808)
- **Cross-blog arc**: From rebel against Nayaks to British ally over a century

### Meenakshi (Queen Regent)
- (Madurai, 1736) - Queen Regent of Madurai Nayaks, requested aid from Chanda Sahib to quell rebellion, was betrayed
- Source: Blog 2 (1700-1761)

### Chanda Sahib
- (Madurai, 1736) - Nephew of Nawab of Arcot, seized Madurai using fake Quran trick
- (Tamil Nadu, 1740) - Captured by invading Marathas
- (Arcot/Trichy, 1749-1752) - French-backed candidate for Nawab, besieged Trichy, beheaded by Manakji

### Anwar-ud-din Khan
- (Arcot, 1740s) - Nawab of Carnatic, sent army to discipline French

- (Ambur, 1749) - Killed at Battle of Ambur

### La Bourdonnais

### Dupleix (Joseph Francois)

## EVENTS

### Cholas Unite the Kaveri Delta
- **Place**: Kaveri Delta (Thanjavur region)
- **Time**: ~900 AD
- **Description**: Aditya and Parantaka Chola unite small warlords through incentives (shared loot from raids), killing the Pallava king, and claiming Sangam-era Chola lineage
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Cholas Defeat Pandyas and Pallavas
- **Place**: Madurai, Tondai Nadu
- **Time**: ~920-940 AD
- **Description**: United Kaveri Delta cartel defeats exhausted Pandyas and Pallavas; Parantaka even tries to raid Sri Lanka
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Rashtrakuta Invasion of Tamil Nadu
- **Place**: Thanjavur → Rameswaram
- **Time**: ~950 AD
- **Description**: Rashtrakutas decimate Chola army, kill crown prince Rajaditya, drive Parantaka from Thanjavur, reach Rameswaram; leave inscription; keep Tondai Nadu as buffer zone
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Fall of the Rashtrakutas
- **Place**: Deccan
- **Time**: ~972 AD
- **Description**: Warlord from northern frontier defeats Rashtrakuta king, ransacks capital; creates power vacuum south of Vindhyas; Cholas had nothing to do with it but benefited
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Chola Peak: Plunder in All Directions
- **Place**: Gangavadi, Bengal coast, Sri Lanka, Chera territory, Indonesia, Malaysia
- **Time**: ~985-1044 AD (Rajaraja + Rajendra)
- **Description**: Without Rashtrakutas to stop them, Cholas plunder north (Rashtrakuta territory), northeast (Bengal coast), south (Pandya + Sri Lanka), west (Chera territory + Arabian Sea), and overseas (Indonesia/Malaysia on merchant ships)
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Chola Revenue System Overhaul
- **Place**: Kaveri Delta, Tondai Nadu, Nadu Nadu
- **Time**: ~970-1014 AD (Uttama → Rajaraja)
- **Description**: Cholas dismantle centuries-old Nadu institutions and local warlord loyalties; establish centralized revenue secretariat cutting out middlemen; farmer's 20 sacks now go directly to Chola coffers instead of split with local warlord and Nadu assembly
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Chola Overseas Raid (Indonesia/Malaysia)
- **Place**: Indonesia, Malaysia (Southeast Asia)
- **Time**: ~1025 AD
- **Description**: Chola army carried on merchant ships raids Southeast Asia; merchants of the 500 guild then harvest camphor and dominate sea routes to China; only known Indian dynasty to conduct trans-oceanic military raids
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Construction of Thanjavur Periya Kovil
- **Place**: Thanjavur
- **Time**: ~1010 AD
- **Description**: Rajaraja builds massive temple; Meikirti inscriptions on walls serve as power projection to distant villages
- Source: Cholas Part 2
- **Coin link**: Chola-era coins (Later Pandya coins mentioned in Madurai blog)

### Later Pandya Revival
- **Place**: Madurai
- **Time**: ~1250 AD
- **Description**: After period of Chola dominance, Pandyas return to power in Madurai; "Later Pandya" period; last Chola King reportedly disappeared into the forest (basis for Aayirathil Oruvan movie)
- Source: Madurai Through the Ages
- **Coin link**: Blog explicitly mentions Later Pandya coins in collection

### Haider Ali's 222 Coin
- **Place**: Mysore
- **Time**: ~1761-1782
- **Description**: Haider Ali's coins feature numeral 222 in Arabic - a cryptographic encoding of his name using Abjad system (Ha=8, Ya=10, Dal=4, Ra=200 = 222)

## KINGDOMS / POWERS

### Chola Empire ("Modern" Cholas)
- (Kaveri Delta → all Tamil Nadu → overseas, ~900-1250 AD) - No connection to Sangam Cholas; co-opted brand name. United Kaveri Delta warlords, defeated Pandyas/Pallavas, bullied Deccan, raided Indonesia/Malaysia. Built Thanjavur Big Temple. Efficient centralized revenue system. Only Tamil dynasty to project power northward and overseas.
- Source: Cholas Part 1, Cholas Part 2
- **Cross-blog arc**: Rise → Rashtrakuta humbling → peak under Rajaraja/Rajendra → decline → Later Pandya revival
- **Coin link**: Chola-era coins likely in collection

### Pandyas
- (Madurai, ancient-1335 AD, then ~1250 revival) - Ancient dynasty; fought Pallavas for 300 years; defeated by Cholas; Later Pandya revival ~1250; fell to Delhi Sultanate 1335
- Source: Cholas Part 1, Madurai Through the Ages
- **Coin link**: Later Pandya coins explicitly mentioned in collection

### Pallavas
- (Tondai Nadu/Kancheepuram, ancient-~900 AD) - Fought Pandyas for centuries; king killed by Aditya Chola; splinter groups persisted
- Source: Cholas Part 1

### Rashtrakutas
- (Deccan, ~750-972 AD) - "Most dangerous gang in India for 200 years"; periodically raided Tamil Nadu; crushed Cholas ~950 AD; fell to northern frontier warlord ~972 AD
- Source: Cholas Part 1, Cholas Part 2

### Madurai Sultanate
- (Madurai, 1335-1375) - Delhi Sultanate breakaway; desecrated temples; driven out by Vijayanagara
- Source: Madurai Through the Ages

### 500 Merchant Guild
- (Tamil Nadu/Southeast Asia, ~1000 AD) - Multinational merchant guild with own flag, private armies, code of conduct; paid customs to Cholas; possibly Cholas were their military arm; harvested camphor from Indonesia, exploited Sri Lankan pearl fisheries
- Source: Cholas Part 2

### Venad / Travancore
- (Kerala, ~1600s+) - Dynasty that became Travancore; issued Thirai Cash coins with deer and battle axe
- Source: A Historical Mistake
- **Coin link**: Thirai Cash double obverse error coin in collection

### Vijayanagara Empire
- (Tamil Nadu, ~1375-1565) - Drove out Madurai Sultanate; restored Pandyas then appointed Nayaka governors; collapsed after Battle of Tallikota
- Source: Blog 1, Madurai Through the Ages

### Chandragiri Nayaks
- (Chandragiri/Tondai Nadu, 1565-~1650) - Fell to Golconda influence
- Source: Blog 1

### Gingee Nayaks
- (Gingee/Nadu Nadu, 1565-~1650) - Fell to Bijapur control
- Source: Blog 1

### Madurai Nayaks
- (Madurai/Pandiya Nadu/Kongu Nadu, 1565-1736) - Gradually declined; lost Ramnad 1707; fell to Nawab 1736
- Source: Blog 1, Blog 2
- **Cross-blog arc**: Independent power → paying tribute → internal strife → absorbed by Nawab

### Thanjavur Nayaks
- (Thanjavur/Kaveri Delta, 1565-1674) - Replaced by Maratha dynasty
- Source: Blog 1

### Golconda Sultanate
- (Tondai Nadu, 1650-~1687) - Expanded into Tamil Nadu, defeated by Mughals
- Source: Blog 1

### Bijapur Sultanate
- (Nadu Nadu/Gingee/Vellore, 1650-~1686) - Expanded into Tamil Nadu, defeated by Mughals

- (Thanjavur, 1674-1800s) - Established by Ekoji; allied with British in Carnatic Wars; Manakji beheaded Chanda Sahib; vassals of Nawab nominally
